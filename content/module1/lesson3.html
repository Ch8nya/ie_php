What Is a Customer Journey Map?

A customer journey map is a visualization that tells the story of a user's experience with a product or service. It compiles user goals, actions, thoughts, and emotions into a narrative timeline. The visualization helps communicate insights to inform design processes and create a shared vision across an organization.

Why Create a Customer Journey Map?

Journey maps should support specific business goals, such as:

  • Shifting to an outside-in, customer-centric perspective
  • Breaking down organizational silos and aligning on the customer experience
  • Assigning internal ownership of key touchpoints
  • Understanding the experiences of specific customer personas
  • Explaining quantitative data by uncovering the "why" behind customer behaviors

Key Elements of Customer Journey Maps

Successful journey maps typically include:

  • Point of view (the persona or "actor")
  • Scenario (the specific experience being mapped)
  • User actions, thoughts, and emotions
  • Touchpoints and channels
  • Insights and internal ownership

The level of detail and scope should align with the map's purpose and intended audience.

Typical Journey Map Structure

Journey maps often follow a model with three key zones:

Zone A: The Lens

  • Defines the persona ("who")
  • Specifies the scenario being examined ("what")

Zone B: The Experience

  • Visualizes the phases of the journey
  • Maps user actions, thoughts, and emotions
  • May include supporting research quotes/videos

Zone C: The Insights

  • Describes pain points and opportunities discovered
  • Assigns internal ownership for journey touchpoints

Rules for Creating Successful Journey Maps

  1. Establish the business goal, audience, and scope upfront
  2. Base the map on qualitative user research and truthful data
  3. Involve stakeholders across groups in a collaborative process
  4. Complete the data synthesis before visualizing the map
  5. Actively engage others with the final journey map artifact

Value for UI/UX Designers

Customer journey maps are invaluable tools for UI/UX designers as they provide a comprehensive understanding of the user's experience across all touchpoints, not just the specific UI or product being designed. This holistic view helps designers:

  • Identify pain points, gaps, and areas for improvement in the overall experience
  • Understand the context, motivations, and emotions driving user behaviors
  • Design solutions that are seamlessly integrated into the entire customer journey
  • Align their designs with the broader customer experience vision and strategy

Design Applications

Customer journey maps can inform and guide various design activities, including:

  • User Research: Maps highlight areas where additional research is needed to fill in knowledge gaps.
  • Information Architecture: Understanding the full journey helps structure content and navigation to support user goals.
  • Interaction Design: Mapping emotions and pain points reveals opportunities for delightful user experiences.
  • Prototyping: Testing prototypes in the context of the complete journey ensures a cohesive experience across channels.

Visualizing the Journey Map

For designers, the visualization of the customer journey map is critical for effectively communicating insights and aligning stakeholders. Techniques for impactful visualizations include:

  • Using intuitive metaphors or analogies to represent the journey stages
  • Incorporating actual user quotes, photos, or videos to "bring the user to life"
  • Highlighting significant moments (both positive and negative) through visual emphasis
  • Allowing for interactivity or layers of detail to explore specific touchpoints

With a well-designed customer journey map, UI/UX designers can effectively advocate for user needs, validate design decisions, and ultimately craft better end-to-end experiences.
